Foundations

What is mental health and why does it matter?

Mental health is a state of well-being in which a person can realise their potential, cope with everyday stress, work productively, and contribute to their community.

It doesn't mean the absence of problems — it means having the tools to face them. Key abilities include:

  • Self-awareness – understanding your own emotions, thoughts, and behaviours
  • Resilience – adapting and recovering from difficulties
  • Social connection – healthy relationships and mutual support
  • Emotional balance – managing intense emotions effectively
  • Meaning & purpose – living in line with your values and goals

People with good mental health can:

  • Build relationships based on trust and respect
  • Handle stress in a healthy way
  • Make balanced and conscious decisions
  • Adapt flexibly to change
  • Contribute meaningfully to their community

The Pillars

Fundamental components of mental health

Each dimension works together to help us live a balanced and fulfilling life.

I

Emotional Balance

Recognising, accepting, and managing emotions, both positive and negative, without being overwhelmed.

II

Healthy Thinking

Observing your thoughts, identifying negative patterns, and replacing them with more balanced perspectives.

III

Resilience

Coping with adversity, adapting to change, and coming back stronger after difficult times.

IV

Social Connections

Healthy relationships and support networks are essential, people need authentic connection to thrive.

V

Meaning & Purpose

The sense that your life has direction and that your actions matter contributes deeply to well-being.

VI

Self-Care

Daily habits — sleep, movement, nutrition, rest, form the physical foundation of mental health.

Scientific Perspective

The PERMA Model – Martin Seligman

Martin Seligman, founder of positive psychology, proposed the PERMA model to describe the ingredients of a truly fulfilling and psychologically healthy life.

According to his theory, lasting happiness comes not from fleeting pleasure but from cultivating five fundamental dimensions. Seligman argues that mental health is not the absence of suffering, but the ability to live meaningfully even in its presence — and these skills can be learned by anyone, at any age.

P
Positive Emotions

Joy, gratitude, hope, serenity

E
Engagement

Flow state and deep absorption in activities

R
Relationships

Authentic connections and social support

M
Meaning

Belonging to something greater than yourself

A
Accomplishment

Goals, competence, and personal progress

Daily Practice

How to improve your mental health

Improving mental health is an ongoing process. Here are practical strategies across the key dimensions.

Mindfulness

  • Practise 10 minutes of meditation or conscious breathing daily
  • Keep a gratitude journal, note 3 good things each day
  • Observe your thoughts without identifying with them

Social Connection

  • Spend quality time with loved ones, away from screens
  • Practise active listening, be fully present in conversations
  • Ask for help when needed, it takes courage, not weakness

Lifestyle

  • 30 minutes of movement daily significantly reduces anxiety
  • Stabilise your sleep schedule, consistent wake and sleep times
  • Limit alcohol, excess caffeine, and evening screen time

Meaning & Purpose

  • Identify your core values and align decisions with them
  • Set small, achievable goals, progress builds motivation
  • Get involved in volunteering or community activities

Professional Support

  • Don't hesitate to see a psychologist or therapist
  • Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) is effective for anxiety and depression
  • Support groups offer connection and mutual understanding

Healthy Boundaries

  • Learn to say "no" without guilt
  • Identify sources of stress and act on those you can control
  • Give yourself permission to rest, without feeling guilty
Authentic social connections are an essential protective factor for mental health

Tests and resources for mental health

Various tools can help you assess your well-being and find the right support.

Standardised Tests

  • PHQ-9 – questionnaire for evaluating depressive symptoms
  • GAD-7 – generalised anxiety assessment scale
  • WHOQOL-BREF – WHO quality of life assessment

Apps & Online Resources

  • Headspace / Calm – guided meditation and mindfulness
  • Woebot – CBT-based emotional support
  • Daylio – mood and activity journal
  • 7 Cups – emotional conversations with trained volunteers

Recommended Books

  • Man's Search for Meaning – Viktor Frankl
  • The Power of Now – Eckhart Tolle
  • Flourish – Martin Seligman
  • Cognitive Therapy of Depression – Aaron Beck
